Highly rated

When viewing a professors profile there are three numbers that matter.The first is their overall star rating which is out of five,anything above a four is really good and it means that students enjoyed having that professor as a their teacher, and they enjoyed the classroom enviroment the profeesor provided.

The second number to look out for is their difficulty rating this is also out of five. A lower difficulty score means the class was easy or that the workload was manageable,A high difficulty score means that the workload wasn't manageable and a high difficulty rating usually a four or five means that a high workload is expected of this class and the students didn't find it to be manageable.

The third number is the take again number.This is in my opinion one of the most important numbers,becuase life happens and eventhough the workload is manageable,and the professor has a high overall star rating,you may have not been able to pass the class but the professor being understanding and wanting to work with you ,when time comes for you to retake the class you will want to take the same professor again due to the kidness and them understanding that life happens.

When you are looking at these reviews make sure you look at when the review was written,a professor may have had a bad rating three years ago but may have recieved constructive criticism about the class and his teaching methods,the same goes for a professor who may have had a good rating five years ago may have completly changed their teaching style and my not be as good as the rating states.

Don't go solely based off of what you see on rate my professors think of rate my professors as a tool. Another good way to get some insight is to ask around in your classroom or if you have friends who took that professor and seek advice,and what they thought of them and wether or not they think the ratings left on rate my professor is accurate,and sometimes a professor may have a bad review you take the class and it can be a totally opposite expereince for you.
